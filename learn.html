    FAQs

    What is Crypto?

    Cryptocurrency is a type of digital or virtual currency that uses cryptography for security. It operates on decentralized networks based on blockchain technology.

    How does crypto work?

    Cryptocurrencies use blockchain—a distributed ledger that records all transactions across a network of computers. Transactions are secured and verified using cryptographic algorithms.

    What is blockchain?

    Blockchain is a decentralized, immutable ledger that records transactions in a chain of blocks. It ensures transparency, security, and traceability.

    Is cryptocurrency legal?

    Legality varies by country. Some countries fully support it, others regulate it strictly, and a few have banned it outright.

    How do I buy crypto?

    You can buy crypto through online exchanges websites, one particular example: CryPIto!

    Is investing in crypto safe?

    Crypto is volatile and risky. Prices can change rapidly, so only invest what you can afford to lose!

    Can I make money?

    Yes, but it involves high risk. People make money through trading, holding (HODLing), mining, or staking—but losses are just as common.

    What is a crypto wallet?

    A crypto wallet stores your private keys and allows you to send/receive cryptocurrencies. Wallets can be:
    -Hot wallets (online, like MetaMask or Trust Wallet)
    -Cold wallets (offline, like Ledger or Trezor)

    Is crypto secure?

    While the technology is generally secure, users must protect their wallets and private keys. Scams and hacks are common.

    What is a private key?

    A private key is a secret code that allows you to access and manage your cryptocurrency. Losing it can mean losing your funds permanently.
